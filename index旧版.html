About Me


I am Jiale Guo, a geospatial data scientist with a BSc in Geography from Chuzhou University, China. My research interests include GIS, GeoAI, spatiotemporal data analysis, and environmental modeling. I have hands-on experience in research and projects involving remote sensing, spatial-temporal analysis, and environmental simulation.

I am passionate about leveraging GIScience, computer science, and data-driven methods (machine learning, GeoAI, spatial-temporal data mining) to address real-world geospatial challenges and support decision-making.

Research Experience

  • Runoff simulation and prediction in the Qinghai Lake basin (SWAT model)
    Research Assistant, Chinese Academy of Sciences (2021-2022)
    - Simulation and prediction of water resources variation in Qinghai Lake Basin.
    - Remote sensing data analysis, spatial data checking, and mapping.
  • Spatiotemporal Evolution and Trend Analysis of Drought Disasters in the Jianghuai Watershed Region
    Undergraduate Thesis, Chuzhou University (2021)
    - Used SPEI index, EOF, M-K test, Wavelet Analysis for drought evaluation and trend analysis.
  • Spatial-temporal pattern mining of PM2.5 pollution in China
    Research Assistant, Geoinformation Lab, Chuzhou University (2020-2021)
    - Used 3D GIS, spatial-temporal cube model, hotspot analysis, clustering, and regression for pollution study.
