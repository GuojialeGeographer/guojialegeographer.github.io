Projects
Runoff simulation and prediction in the Qinghai Lake basin based on the SWAT model

Timeline: 10.2021 - 06.2022

Research project at Chinese Academy of Sciences focusing on simulating and predicting multi-year variation rules of water resources in the Qinghai Lake Basin using the SWAT model. The work involved leveraging remote sensing for data analysis and interpretation.

Spatial-temporal Evolution and Trend Analysis of Drought Disasters in the Jianghuai Watershed Region

Timeline: 01.2021 - 05.2021

Undergraduate thesis research focused on computing the Standardized Precipitation Evapotranspiration Index (SPEI) as an indicator for drought evaluation. The project explored spatial-temporal change characteristics and future trends of meteorological drought using various analysis methods including EOF, M-K test, Wavelet Analysis, and Spatial Analysis.

Spatial-temporal pattern mining of PM2.5 pollution in China and analysis of its driving mechanisms

Timeline: 01.2020 - 06.2021

Research conducted at the Geoinformation Lab of Chuzhou University, utilizing 3D GIS technology and the space-time cube model. The project employed spatial-temporal hotspot data analysis methods to examine the evolution trends and distribution patterns of PM2.5 in China, and analyzed impacts of natural and socio-economic factors using Geodetector and Geographically Weighted Regression models.

On the Remote Sensing Classification of Tree Species Based on Multi-scale Feature Transfer Learning

Timeline: 06.2018 - 12.2019

Research conducted at the Remote Sensing and Big Data Analysis Lab of Chuzhou University. The project utilized QuickBird high-resolution remote sensing imagery to develop a tree species classification model with high accuracy. The methodology combined multi-scale segmentation algorithms and transfer learning to achieve precision identification and classification of forest tree species.
